# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers of **ReqEngine** pledge to making participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to creating a professional, inclusive, and collaborative environment that promotes learning, innovation, and excellence in software engineering.

---

## ‚ú® Our Standards

### Positive Behavior
Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

#### üó£Ô∏è Communication
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language** in all interactions
- **Being respectful** of differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Providing constructive feedback** on code, designs, and ideas
- **Asking questions** when clarification is needed
- **Sharing knowledge** and helping others learn

#### ü§ù Collaboration
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism** and code reviews
- **Focusing on what is best** for the ReqEngine project and community
- **Showing empathy** towards other community members
- **Recognizing contributions** from all team members
- **Supporting newcomers** and helping them get started

#### üíº Professionalism
- **Maintaining academic integrity** in all coursework-related activities
- **Respecting deadlines** and project milestones
- **Being accountable** for assigned tasks and responsibilities
- **Communicating proactively** about challenges or delays
- **Following established development processes** and guidelines

### Unacceptable Behavior
Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

#### üö´ Harassment & Discrimination
- The use of **sexualized language or imagery** and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- **Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks
- **Public or private harassment** of any kind
- **Discriminatory jokes or comments** based on personal characteristics
- **Intimidation or threats** towards other participants

#### üì¢ Unprofessional Conduct
- **Publishing others' private information**, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- **Disruptive behavior** in meetings, presentations, or collaborative sessions
- **Plagiarism or academic dishonesty** in any form
- **Deliberately introducing bugs** or malicious code
- **Violating confidentiality** agreements or sharing sensitive information

#### üö® Technical Misconduct
- **Submitting malicious pull requests** or code that could harm systems
- **Attempting to gain unauthorized access** to systems or data
- **Spamming issues or discussions** with irrelevant content
- **Deliberately breaking builds** or CI/CD pipelines
- **Ignoring security best practices** that could compromise the project

## üõ°Ô∏è Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers
Project maintainers (course instructors and team leads) are responsible for:

- **Clarifying standards** of acceptable behavior
- **Taking appropriate and fair corrective action** in response to unacceptable behavior
- **Removing, editing, or rejecting** comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- **Banning temporarily or permanently** any contributor for behaviors deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Providing guidance** to help contributors improve their collaboration skills

### All Contributors
Every contributor is responsible for:

- **Following this Code of Conduct** in all project interactions
- **Reporting violations** when they observe them
- **Supporting** a positive and inclusive community culture
- **Mentoring** new contributors when possible
- **Leading by example** in professional behavior

## üåç Scope

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment** - Reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Investigation** - Fair and thorough review of the situation
3. **Response** - Appropriate action taken based on findings
4. **Follow-up** - Ongoing monitoring to ensure resolution

### Confidentiality
The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding:
- **Reporter identity** (unless waived by reporter)
- **Investigation details** not relevant to resolution
- **Personal information** shared during the process

## ‚öñÔ∏è Consequences

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

### Possible Actions Include:

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning
- **Private warning** explaining the violation
- **Guidance** on expected behavior going forward
- **Documentation** of the incident for future reference

#### üîÑ Temporary Restrictions
- **Temporary suspension** from project activities
- **Supervised contributions** for a specified period
- **Required training** or mentoring sessions

#### üö´ Permanent Removal
- **Permanent ban** from the project and community
- **Removal of contribution credits** (in severe cases)
- **Notification to academic authorities** if academic integrity is violated

### Academic Consequences
In addition to project-level consequences, violations may result in:
- **Academic disciplinary action** through NCSU policies
- **Grade penalties** for coursework-related violations
- **Referral to Honor Board** for academic integrity issues
